Projects

Research Trainee at UNESCO-RCB

Currently immersed in structural biology as a Research Trainee at UNESCO-RCB, Faridabad, under the guidance of Prof. Dr. Deepak T. Nair. My primary focus is understanding the mysteries of the clamp loader complex from Mycobacterium tuberculosis using the cutting-edge Cryo-EM technique, with a touch of finesse from the CryoSPARC software.

MSc Dissertation - "Cloning, purification, and reconstitution of the multi-subunit clamp loader"

Recently completed a captivating dissertation project under the mentorship of Prof. Dr. Deepak T. Nair. Successfully cloned genes, danced through chromatography techniques for purification, and orchestrated the reunion of subunits into a formidable protein complex. Let's not forget the captivating negative stain electron microscopy for that structural flair!

Summer Internship Adventurer at Kalyani University

During a short but impactful summer stint at Kalyani University, West Bengal, under the keen eye of Dr. Arunima Biswas, I dived into the world of elevated phosphodiesterase expression and its dance with cAMP levels in breast cancer stem cells. Mastering Western blotting, cell culture, and cytotoxicity assays, I uncovered the secrets of cellular communications.

Field Explorer Extraordinaire at Sagar Island

Venturing into the wilds of Sagar Island, West Bengal, I participated in a biodiversity expedition organized by the Zoology Dept. Our mission: unravel the secrets of Sagar Island's diverse ecosystem. Armed with identification skills and geotagging prowess, we left no leaf unturned.

Scientific Wordsmith at Ramakrishna Mission Vidyamandira
